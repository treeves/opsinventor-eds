Typhoon Durian hits the Philippines on Yahoo! News Photos

The Red Cross has already recorded a death toll of at least 406, with 398 others missing, based on figures provided by mayors of devastated towns in the eastern Philippines. No survivors are reported on farmland buried by volcanic mud, debris and boulders and hopes for finding any have virtually vanished.

The typhoon was the fourth big storm to hit the islands in four months. It buffeted the Mayon volcano with winds of up to 165 mph (265 km/h), causing ash and boulders to cascade down in walls of black mud that swamped villages.

Most of the deaths were in Albay province, where 52 tonnes of relief goods, including medicines and body bags, have been flown in. Bodies were buried in mass graves over the weekend. (AP)
